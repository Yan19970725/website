Table tennis     (ball sports)

Table tennis, known as China's "national ball", is a world-famous ball sport, including offense, confrontation and defense.

Table tennis originated in Britain. One of the "pingpong" originated in 1900 and was named after the sound of "Ping Pong" when hitting. In mainland China, "table tennis" is its official name, as well as Hong Kong and Macao, China. In January 1926, an international table tennis competition was held in Berlin, Germany, in which 64 male athletes from nine countries participated. In December of the same year, the International Table Tennis Federation was officially established and named the European Championship in London the first World Table Tennis Championship.

Table tennis organizations include the International Table Tennis Federation, the Asian Table Tennis Federation and the China Table Tennis Association.

Project History

Origin of the project

Table tennis originated in England. At the end of the 19th century, tennis was popular in Europe, but due to the limitations of the venue and weather, some British college students moved tennis indoors, using the dining table as the table, making nets, using parchment as rackets, and playing around the table.

Project changes

In 1903, the British Goode invented the rubber racket, which strongly promoted the development of table tennis technology.

In 1926, the International Table Tennis Federation (ITTF) was officially established and decided to hold the first World Table Tennis Championships.

From 1926 to 1951, 18 World Table Tennis Championships were held. Most players from all over the world used rubber rackets with cylindrical particles on the surface. When hitting the ball, elasticity and friction are increased, which can make the ball rotate to a certain extent, so there is a defensive way to cut down the spin ball. This style of play has been popular in Europe for a long time, and many athletes have used this method to win the world championship.
